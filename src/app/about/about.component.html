Eliza Siegel is a New Jersey-based interdisciplinary writer and artist who recently graduated from Barnard College of Columbia University with a BA in American Studies. She is the recipient of the 2020 John Demos Prize in American Studies and the 2020 Women and Gender Studies Prize administered by Columbia University’s Institute for Research on Women, Gender, and Sexuality (read her interview with IRWGS here). Most days, she can be found writing scathing political satire for The Rational, or else digging up carrots on a small New Jersey farm. In the past, she worked as an editorial intern at the Prague-based newsroom Transitions Online. Her poetry and editorial writing has appeared in Voices and Visions Literary Journal, New York’s Best Emerging Poets: An Anthology, [sic.] Literary Journal, Roaches Literary Journal, Fellow Voices, and more.
